ABOUT // OPERATOR // MANIFESTO

STILL
MOVING
FORWARD

Twenty-five years. Not in a straight line — nothing worth doing ever is. From the chaos of a breaking news floor at 2 AM to the engineered silence of a scripted set waiting for magic. From inside a cage while two fighters try to end each other, to a studio where legends argue about who was greater. Every shot is the same problem: find the truth in the frame before the moment disappears.

A Steadicam is not a camera system. It's a philosophy. The rig lives between you and the shot — isolating intention from tremor, separating what you mean from what your body does under pressure. After 25 years, you stop operating it. You wear it. You think through it.

The industry has changed three times over. I've changed with it, and sometimes ahead of it. Reality television taught me to never stop moving and never stop anticipating. Live sports taught me that perfection is the enemy of the essential. Scripted work taught me that preparation is invisible when it's working. News taught me that nothing matters except what happens next.

I'm not a legend. I'm still grinding. Still evolving. Still finding better ways to carry the weight.

// Philosophy

The best shot is the one nobody notices because it felt so inevitable they thought it was real life.

// On Steadicam

The rig is just a tool. The operator is the instrument. Separate your muscle from your intention and magic happens.

// On Live Sports

You can't manufacture a Shaq laugh. You can only be positioned to catch it when it happens.
